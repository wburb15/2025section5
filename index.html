The Ohio State Buckeyes football team represents Ohio State University in Columbus and stands as one of the most dominant programs in college football.

Competing in the NCAA Division I FBS as a member of the Big Ten Conference, the team has built a legacy of excellence since its founding in 1890.

The Buckeyes play their home games in Ohio Stadium, famously known as “The Shoe,” a massive venue that seats over 100,000 fans and creates one of the most electric game-day atmospheres in the country.

Throughout their history, the Buckeyes have captured numerous national championships, produced a long list of All-Americans, Heisman Trophy winners, and NFL talent, and have consistently finished among the top programs in national rankings.

Beyond their on-field success, the Buckeyes are defined by their rich culture and traditions: from the beloved Brutus Buckeye mascot to the Ohio State Marching Band’s celebrated halftime performances and the iconic “Script Ohio.”

One of the most defining elements of Buckeye identity is their historic rivalry with the Michigan Wolverines, a matchup often referred to as “The Game,” which is widely considered one of the greatest rivalries in American sports.

Today, Ohio State continues to be a powerhouse known for elite recruiting, innovative coaching, and a passionate fanbase that fills the stadium year after year, reinforcing the team’s place as a central force in college football.
